In Plasencia, as in other cities in the Kingdom of Castile and Leon, residents were subject to multiple forms of annual taxation.  The most important of these were church taxes on goods and livestock (diezmos), royal sales taxes (alcalaba), and royal toll taxes for goods passing through the city gates (portazgo.) In Plasencia, the portazgo was assessed on a wide assortment of items.
