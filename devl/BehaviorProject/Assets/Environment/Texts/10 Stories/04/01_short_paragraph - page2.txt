To appreciate the value of 62,000 maravedis, consider that the average salary of a church canon was just 185 maravedis a year.

The testament also makes clear that, because Diego Gonzalez de Carvajal had married three times, he was worried about the distribution of his wealth to no less than twelve children by his three wives. In fact, the proper distribution of inheritance and returning what each spouse had brought to their marriage was a big concern. In a telling example, Diego willed, “And because...Teresa Rodriguez, my wife, brought and gave to me a small amount of silver that I had made into a rounded embellishment with the figure of a pine tree for my sword, I will that it be for the said Teresa…because it was made with her silver.” In this way, a wealthy knight struggled to settle his last affairs.
